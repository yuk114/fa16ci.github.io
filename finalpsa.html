Diversity in Fashion Does Not Exist

The issue is white models have dominated the catwalks while models of color are few and far between. Designers like Francisco Costa formerly of Calvin Klein Collection and Demna Gvasalia of Vetments and Balenciaga have named as those who do not use any models of color and have an all white line up. Up until the Diveristy Coalition - a group formed by casting director and legendary model of the 1970s Bethann Hardison with memebers like Naomi Campbell and Iman - no one had really called out designers for the apparent racism in the industry. After this exposure by the Diversity Coalition, Francisco Costa had no choise but to put models of color on the runway, something he had not done up until the Spring Summer 2014 show. Fashion website, thefashionspot.com analyzed 151 major shows of the Spring Summer 2017 season, in New York (48), London (31), Milan (30), and Paris (42), to see exactly how committed top designers are to representing a diverse group of models on the runway. They counted black, white, Asian, Middle Eastern, Latina and female models of ambiguous ethnicity and found that the odds of women of color showing up on the runway are still extremely slim. - The Fashion Spot
